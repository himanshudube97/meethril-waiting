# Cold Outreach Templates

Four archetypes, four templates. Personalize the `[bracketed bits]` before sending — the bracketed parts are non-negotiable. Generic outreach gets ignored or marked as spam.

## Universal rules

1. **First DM/email = no link.** Build a tiny relationship first. Link comes in turn 2 or 3.
2. **Reference one specific thing they made.** Not "love your content" — name a post.
3. **Send 5–10 a day, max.** Mass outreach gets you flagged.
4. **Wait at least 7 days before following up.** Once. Then leave them alone.
5. **Don't pitch. Invite.** You're showing them something they might love. They're doing you a favor by even reading.
6. **Lowercase, short, warm.** Mirrors the brand voice. "hi" not "Hi!". "thank you" not "Thanks so much!!"

## Archetype 1: Bullet journal / journal aesthetic creators

> They post spread photos, washi tape collections, slow morning journaling reels. Audience: 5k–200k. Best on Instagram + TikTok DMs.

### First DM (no link)

> hi [name] —
>
> i found your [specific post — e.g. "october cover spread with the moth illustration"] last week and have been thinking about it since. the way you let the page breathe is rare.
>
> i'm building a quiet journaling app — no streaks, no AI, no notifications, just a soft place to write — and i wondered if you'd be open to seeing an early peek before it opens in may. i'd love your eyes on it before anyone else's.
>
> no obligation. just a "yes, send it" or a "no thanks" and i'll leave you in peace. ♥
>
> [your name]

### Follow-up (only if they say yes)

> thank you. ♥
>
> here's the early site: meethril.com
>
> if you scroll through and any of it speaks to you, i'd love to hear what. and if it doesn't, i'd love to hear that too — honest reactions are more useful than kind ones.
>
> if you ever wanted to share it with anyone, here's a personal link that i'd see come through: [their personal ref link]
>
> no pressure either way. just glad you're seeing it.

## Archetype 2: Slow living / cottagecore / quiet life

> They post candlelit mornings, slow afternoons, books, tea, fields. Audience: often very engaged, smaller (3k–80k). Best on Instagram.

### First DM

> hi [name] —
>
> [specific compliment — e.g. "the post about the rosehip syrup last sunday felt like a long exhale"]. thank you for that.
>
> i'm building something i think your corner of the internet might love — a quiet journaling app, made for people who are tired of being optimized. it opens in may.
>
> would it be alright if i sent you a peek before it opens? i'd love your honest reaction before anyone else's. genuinely no pressure if not.
>
> [your name]

### Follow-up (after yes)

> here it is: meethril.com
>
> the words are slow on purpose. tell me anything you think.
>
> and a personal link in case you ever feel moved to share it: [their personal ref link]

## Archetype 3: Stationery / penpal / analog letter writing

> They review pens, post letter spreads, run penpal exchanges. Often have a Substack or YouTube. Audience: niche but extremely loyal.

### First DM / email

>
> i've been reading [their work — e.g. "your substack on the lost art of letter-keeping"] and felt like i should reach out.

>
> no obligation. just thought you'd be one of the few people who'd actually get it.
>
> [your name]

### Follow-up (after yes)

> here's the site: meethril.com
>
> the letters feature opens to early-access folks first. i'd love to hear what you think — not as a creator, just as someone who keeps letters.
>
> a personal link if you ever want to share: [their personal ref link]

## Archetype 4: Mental health / mindful journaling / therapist-adjacent

> They post about anxiety, grief, journaling-for-wellbeing. Often verified, careful with brand alignment. Be especially honest about what the product is NOT.

### First DM / email

> hi [name] —
>
> i appreciate the way you talk about [specific topic — e.g. "the difference between processing and ruminating in last tuesday's post"]. it's a clarity i don't see often.
>
> i've been building a journaling app that's deliberately quiet — no streaks, no engagement loops, no AI generating prompts, no wellness scoring. i don't want to make journaling more compulsive than it already is for some people. it opens in may.
>
> i'm reaching out to people who think carefully about this space because i'd love your eyes on it before launch — to tell me what's good, and especially to tell me what's not.
>
> would you be open to a peek?
>
> [your name]

### Follow-up (after yes)

> thank you. here's the site: meethril.com
>
> the things i'd most love your honest read on:
> — the encryption story (does it feel reassuring or technical?)
> — the "no streaks / no AI" framing (does it land, or does it feel like a negative pitch?)
> — anything you'd actively warn a client away from
>
> a personal link in case you ever want to share: [their personal ref link]

## Email-specific templates (when you only have email, not a DM channel)

Same structure as DMs, but:
- Subject line should be **a phrase, not a pitch**
- Format with a single blank line between paragraphs
- Sign with first name + meethril.com (in your sig, not the body)

### Subject line options (use one)

- `a quiet thing i think you'd like`
- `something built for people who keep letters`
- `[their first name] — a peek before it opens`
- `noticed your work on [specific] — would love your eyes on something`

### Email body (Archetype 1 example)

> Hi [name],
>
> Found your [specific post] last week and have been thinking about it since.
>
> I'm building a quiet journaling app — no streaks, no AI, no notifications — and would love your eyes on it before it opens in May. Would you be open to a peek?
>
> No pressure either way.
>
> — [Your name]

## What to do when they reply

| Reply | Action |
|---|---|
| "yes, send it" | Send Follow-up. **Don't** add a sales pitch. |
| "looks cool, will check it out" | Reply with the link, one sentence of context, and a personal share link. Move on. |
| "i'd be open to a paid post" | "i'd love to but the budget is small. would a free preview work? i can revisit when launch budget exists." |
| Silence after 7 days | One follow-up. "hi [name] — circling back gently. no worries either way." |
| Silence after follow-up | Move on. Don't email again. |
| "not for me, thanks" | "thank you for being honest. ♥" — that's it. |

## Tracking (do this manually for the first month)

Spreadsheet with columns:
- Name | Platform | Handle | Followers | Date sent | Reply? | Date reply | Posted? | URL of post | Signups attributed (grep `ref=` in logs)

This tells you which archetypes convert best so you can double down.
