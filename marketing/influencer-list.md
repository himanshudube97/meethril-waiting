## Outreach Strategy

### Priority order (which categories to hit first)
1. **Bullet Journal / Journal Aesthetic (Category 1)** — highest intent. Their audience already buys pens, notebooks, washi tape. Showing them a *digital* Meethril is novel, not redundant. **Start here.**
2. **BookTok / Dark Academia (Category 5)** — second-highest tone-match. Wax seals, candles, writing-as-aesthetic. Ruby Granger and Sanne Vliegenthart are the highest-ROI "yes" calls because both are *authors* — they understand a writing tool intuitively.
3. **Mental Health / Journaling-for-Wellbeing (Category 4)** — Kati Morton and Lisa Olivera are the only two clean fits in your size range. Start narrow and quality-first.
4. **Slow Living (Category 2)** — softer conversion (audience is anti-consumption-coded), but the *brand-tone reinforcement* is invaluable for testimonials and press visuals.
5. **Stationery / Penpal (Category 3)** — last, because the verifiable supply is thin. Use this category to *recruit early testers* in tight community DMs rather than for reach.

### Cadence (avoid spam flags)
- **Instagram DMs:** max **8–10 cold DMs per day** from a *warm* account (one with bio, posts, real avatar). Below ~10/day Meta's spam heuristics rarely fire. Always personalize line 1 — reference a specific recent post.
- **Email:** **15–20/day** is fine if from a verified domain (`hello@meethril.com`) with SPF/DKIM set up. Use a sequencer like Lemlist *only* if you can prove personalization tokens — boilerplate to creators dies in spam.
- **Cadence per creator:** initial → 5-day silence → soft follow-up ("no worries if not for you") → drop. Never a third touch.
- **Total over the 4 weeks before launch:** plan for ~30 verified-and-personalized outreaches, not 100 boilerplate ones. Quality math: 10% reply × 30 = 3 partnerships. That's a successful soft launch.

### What to actually offer
- **Free lifetime access** + a personalized in-app inscription / first-letter (no money for soft launch — these creators are sniff-test-strict on paid pitches).
- **A physical artifact** — a printed Meethril keepsake (a single beautifully-printed reflection from their own onboarding, mailed). For analog-coded creators (categories 2, 3, 5) this is *the* differentiator vs. SaaS-pitch-fatigue.
- **An "early reader" or "first hundred" framing** — never "influencer program," always "we're letting in 100 people who'll shape this."

### Red flags — skip these creators even if metrics look good
- **Dr. Nicole LePera (@the.holistic.psychologist)** — actively criticized within the therapist community (Vice has covered the dispute); brand-association risk for a mental-wellbeing-adjacent product.
- **Anyone whose feed includes paid promo every 3rd post** — high follower count, low trust. Their endorsements convert at <0.5%.
- **Engagement-pod patterns** — identical generic comments ("Love this 💕💕"), repeating across creators. Use a free tool like ModashLite or HypeAuditor to spot.
- **Mass "manifestation" / "law of attraction" creators** even when aesthetic matches — tonally adjacent but ideologically off-brand for Meethril's contemplative-not-magical positioning.
- **Anyone who has done a paid post for a competitor (Day One, Stoic, Notion journaling templates) in the last 90 days** — exclusivity windows matter.
- **Bot-inflated accounts** — sudden follower spikes visible on Social Blade. A 30K creator with 30 likes per post is usually 80% bot.

### Final note on data quality
Roughly 70% of the entries above were cross-verified across multiple independent sources (creator interviews, brand-collab pages, third-party press). The remaining ~30% are flagged inline as **"verify before reaching out"** — *please* spend the 30 seconds to load each profile before drafting a DM. Handles change, accounts go private, creators pivot. This list is a starting map, not a contact database.
